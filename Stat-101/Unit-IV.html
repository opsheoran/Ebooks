4.1 Introduction to Moments

In statistics, Moments are a set of statistical measures used to describe the characteristics of a distribution. Just as the mean and standard deviation describe the center and spread, moments provide a systematic way to quantify the shape, skewness, and peakedness of the data.

Statistics में, Moments statistical measures का एक set है जिसका उपयोग distribution की विशेषताओं (characteristics) को describe करने के लिए किया जाता है। जिस तरह mean और standard deviation center और spread को describe करते हैं, moments data की shape, skewness और peakedness को quantify करने का एक व्यवस्थित (systematic) तरीका प्रदान करते हैं।

Definition of Moments

The \(r^{th}\) moment of a variable \(X\) about any point \(A\) is the arithmetic mean of the \(r^{th}\) powers of the deviations of the observations from \(A\).

किसी भी point \(A\) के परितः (about) variable \(X\) का \(r^{th}\) moment, \(A\) से observations के deviations की \(r^{th}\) powers का arithmetic mean होता है।

\[ \mu'_r = \frac{1}{N} \sum f_i (X_i - A)^r \]

4.2 Central Moments

Central Moments (also called Moments about the Mean) are calculated by taking deviations from the arithmetic mean (\(\bar{X}\)). These are the most important moments as they describe the internal structure of the distribution independent of the origin.

Central Moments (जिन्हें Mean के परितः moments भी कहा जाता है) arithmetic mean (\(\bar{X}\)) से deviations लेकर calculate किए जाते हैं। ये सबसे महत्वपूर्ण moments हैं क्योंकि ये origin से स्वतंत्र distribution के internal structure को describe करते हैं।

Formula for Central Moments (\(\mu_r\))

\[ \mu_r = \frac{1}{N} \sum f_i (X_i - \bar{X})^r \]

Important Properties:

  • \(\mu_0 = 1\) (Always)
  • \(\mu_1 = 0\) (The first central moment is always zero)
  • \(\mu_2 = \sigma^2\) (The second central moment is the Variance)

4.6 Skewness

Skewness means 'lack of symmetry'. We study skewness to get an idea about the shape of the curve that can be drawn with the given data. If in a distribution, Mean = Median = Mode, then that distribution is called a symmetrical distribution.

Skewness का अर्थ है 'symmetry की कमी' (lack of symmetry)। हम skewness का अध्ययन दिए गए data के साथ खींचे जा सकने वाले curve के shape के बारे में विचार प्राप्त करने के लिए करते हैं। यदि किसी distribution में, Mean = Median = Mode है, तो उस distribution को symmetrical distribution कहा जाता है।

Karl Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness

\[ S_k = \frac{\text{Mean} - \text{Mode}}{\sigma} \]

Or if Mode is ill-defined:

\[ S_k = \frac{3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median})}{\sigma} \]

Example: Calculation of Karl-Pearson's Coefficient

Data of monthly income of farmers in a district of Haryana:

Income (in thousands of ₹) 10-15 15-20 20-25 25-30 30-35 35-40
Number of Farmers 25 35 40 30 15 5

Solution:

Mean = ₹22,500, Median = ₹21,800, \(\sigma\) = ₹6,200

Karl-Pearson's \(S_k = \frac{3(22,500 - 21,800)}{6,200} = 0.34\)

Since the coefficient is positive, the distribution is positively skewed.

4.7 Kurtosis

Kurtosis is used to describe the peakedness of a curve. It measures how much more peaked or more flat-topped the distribution is compared to the normal curve.

Kurtosis का उपयोग curve की peakedness को describe करने के लिए किया जाता है। यह मापता है कि normal curve की तुलना में distribution कितना अधिक peaked या अधिक flat-topped है।

Types of Kurtosis

  • Mesokurtic: Normal curve (\(\beta_2 = 3\))
  • Leptokurtic: More peaked (\(\beta_2 > 3\))
  • Platykurtic: Flatter (\(\beta_2 < 3\))
\[ \beta_2 = \frac{\mu_4}{\mu_2^2} \quad \text{and} \quad \gamma_2 = \beta_2 - 3 \]

1. In a symmetrical distribution, Mean = 50. What are the values of Median and Mode?

A) 50 and 50
B) 40 and 60
C) 0 and 100
D) Cannot be determined

Correct Answer: A) 50 and 50

In a symmetrical distribution, Mean = Median = Mode.

2. If \(\beta_2 > 3\), the distribution is:

A) Mesokurtic
B) Leptokurtic
C) Platykurtic
D) Symmetrical

Correct Answer: B) Leptokurtic
