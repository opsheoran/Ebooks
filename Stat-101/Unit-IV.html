4.1 Introduction & Skewness

4.1 परिचय और Skewness

Measures of central tendency tell us where the values are concentrated, and dispersion tells us how they are spread. However, they do not tell us about the symmetry of the distribution. Skewness means 'lack of symmetry'. In a symmetrical distribution, the values are evenly distributed on both sides of the mean.

Central tendency के measures हमें बताते हैं कि values कहाँ concentrated हैं, और dispersion बताता है कि वे कैसे spread हैं। लेकिन, वे distribution की symmetry के बारे में नहीं बताते। Skewness का अर्थ है 'symmetry की कमी'। एक symmetrical distribution में, values mean के दोनों ओर समान रूप से वितरित होती हैं।

Symmetrical
Positive Skew
Negative Skew

Examples from Real Life

Agricultural Income in Haryana

A survey of farmers' monthly income in a Haryana district shows that most farmers have low to moderate income, while a few large farmers have exceptionally high income. This results in a Positively Skewed distribution where the Mean is pulled higher by the few extreme values.

हरियाणा के एक जिले में किसानों की monthly income का survey बताता है कि अधिकांश किसानों की आय कम से मध्यम है, जबकि कुछ बड़े किसानों की आय बहुत अधिक है। इसका परिणाम एक Positively Skewed distribution होता है जहाँ Mean कुछ extreme values के कारण ऊपर खिंच जाता है।

Height of Students

Heights of students in a university are usually Symmetrical. If the average height is 170 cm, there are roughly as many students at 160 cm as there are at 180 cm.

Types of Skewness

Skewness के प्रकार

  • Zero Skew (Symmetrical): Mean = Median = Mode. The curve is bell-shaped.
  • Positive Skew (Right-skewed): The right tail is longer. Mean > Median > Mode.
  • Negative Skew (Left-skewed): The left tail is longer. Mean < Median < Mode.
  • Zero Skew (Symmetrical): Mean = Median = Mode. Curve bell-shaped होता है।
  • Positive Skew (Right-skewed): Right tail लंबी होती है। Mean > Median > Mode.
  • Negative Skew (Left-skewed): Left tail लंबी होती है। Mean < Median < Mode.

4.2 Coefficients of Skewness

4.2 Skewness के Coefficients

Relative measures of skewness are called coefficients. They are independent of units.

Skewness के relative measures को coefficients कहा जाता है। ये units से स्वतंत्र होते हैं।

Karl Pearson's Coefficient (\(S_k\))

\[ \begin{aligned} S_k &= \frac{\text{Mean} - \text{Mode}}{\sigma} \\ &\text{If Mode is not clear:} \\ S_k &= \frac{3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median})}{\sigma} \end{aligned} \]

Bowley's Coefficient (\(S_B\))

Based on Quartiles. Range is -1 to +1.

\[ S_B = \frac{Q_3 + Q_1 - 2\text{Median}}{Q_3 - Q_1} \]

Example 1: Grouped Data (Karl Pearson)

Following is the frequency distribution of plant heights in a farm. Calculate Karl Pearson's coefficient of skewness.

Height (cm)70-8080-9090-100100-110110-120
No. of Plants51525105

Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{Mean } (\bar{X}) &= \frac{\sum f m}{N} = \frac{75(5) + 85(15) + 95(25) + 105(10) + 115(5)}{60} \\ &= \frac{375 + 1275 + 2375 + 1050 + 575}{60} = \frac{5650}{60} \approx 94.17 \text{ cm} \\ \text{Mode} &= L + \frac{f_1 - f_0}{2f_1 - f_0 - f_2} \times h \\ &= 90 + \frac{25 - 15}{50 - 15 - 10} \times 10 \\ &= 90 + \frac{10}{25} \times 10 = 90 + 4 = 94 \text{ cm} \\ \text{Variance } (\sigma^2) &= \frac{\sum f m^2}{N} - (\bar{X})^2 \approx 103.47 \implies \sigma \approx 10.17 \\ S_k &= \frac{94.17 - 94}{10.17} = \frac{0.17}{10.17} \approx 0.017 \end{aligned} \]

Interpretation: Negligible positive skewness. Distribution is almost symmetrical.

4.3 Kurtosis: Concept & Coefficient

4.3 Kurtosis: Concept और Coefficient

Kurtosis refers to the peakedness of a distribution relative to the Normal Curve.

Kurtosis Normal Curve के सापेक्ष distribution की peakedness को संदर्भित करता है।

Mesokurtic (\(\beta_2 = 3\))
Leptokurtic (\(\beta_2 > 3\))
Platykurtic (\(\beta_2 < 3\))

Measure of Kurtosis (\(\beta_2\))

\[ \beta_2 = \frac{\mu_4}{\mu_2^2} \quad \text{and} \quad \gamma_2 = \beta_2 - 3 \]

4.4 Theory of Attributes & Notations

4.4 Theory of Attributes और Notations

Attributes are qualitative characteristics like 'literacy', 'gender', or 'disease'. We cannot measure them numerically, but we can count their frequency.

Attributes qualitative विशेषताएं हैं जैसे 'साक्षरता', 'लिंग', या 'रोग'। हम उन्हें numerical रूप से नहीं माप सकते, लेकिन हम उनकी frequency गिन सकते हैं।

Symbolic Notations

  • Capital Letters (A, B, C): Presence of an attribute.
  • Greek Letters (\(\alpha, \beta, \gamma\)): Absence of an attribute.
  • (A): Frequency of attribute A.
  • (\(\alpha\)): Frequency of non-A individuals.

4.5 Dichotomy & Class Frequencies

4.5 Dichotomy और Class Frequencies

Dividing a population into two groups based on an attribute is called Dichotomy.

किसी attribute के आधार पर population को दो समूहों में विभाजित करना Dichotomy कहलाता है।

Frequency Relationships (2 Attributes)

\[ \begin{aligned} N &= (A) + (\alpha) = (B) + (\beta) \\ (A) &= (AB) + (A\beta) \\ (B) &= (AB) + (\alpha B) \\ (\alpha) &= (\alpha B) + (\alpha\beta) \\ (\beta) &= (A\beta) + (\alpha\beta) \\ N &= (AB) + (A\beta) + (\alpha B) + (\alpha\beta) \end{aligned} \]

4.6 Consistency of Data

4.6 Data की Consistency

Data is consistent if all class frequencies are non-negative.

Example: Checking Consistency

Given \(N=500, (A)=300, (B)=250, (AB)=40\). Is this data consistent?

Check \((\alpha\beta)\):

\[ \begin{aligned} (\alpha\beta) &= N - (A) - (B) + (AB) \\ &= 500 - 300 - 250 + 40 = -10 \end{aligned} \]

Since \((\alpha\beta) = -10\), which is negative, the data is Inconsistent.

4.7 Independence & Association

4.7 Independence और Association

Condition for Independence:

\[ (AB) = \frac{(A) \times (B)}{N} \]

If \((AB) > \text{Expected}\), it is Positive Association. If \((AB) < \text{Expected}\), it is Negative Association.

4.8 Yule's Coefficients (Q & Y)

4.8 Yule's Coefficients (Q और Y)

Coefficient of Association (\(Q\))

\[ Q = \frac{(AB)(\alpha\beta) - (A\beta)(\alpha B)}{(AB)(\alpha\beta) + (A\beta)(\alpha B)} \]

Numerical Example: Association

In a group of 200 people, 120 are males (A). 80 people are smokers (B). 60 male smokers (AB) were found. Calculate Yule's coefficient of association between males and smoking.

Solution:

\(N=200, (A)=120, (B)=80, (AB)=60\)

\[ \begin{aligned} (A\beta) &= (A) - (AB) = 120 - 60 = 60 \\ (\alpha B) &= (B) - (AB) = 80 - 60 = 20 \\ (\alpha\beta) &= N - (A) - (B) + (AB) = 200 - 120 - 80 + 60 = 60 \\ Q &= \frac{(60)(60) - (60)(20)}{(60)(60) + (60)(20)} = \frac{3600 - 1200}{3600 + 1200} \\ &= \frac{2400}{4800} = 0.5 \end{aligned} \]

There is a positive association (0.5) between males and smoking.

❓ Multiple Choice Questions

1. Which of the following is true for a symmetrical distribution?

A) Mean > Median
B) Mean < Mode
C) Mean = Median = Mode
D) \(S_k = 1\)
Correct Answer: C) Mean = Median = Mode

2. For a Mesokurtic distribution, the value of \(\beta_2\) is:

A) 0
B) < 3
C) 3
D) > 3
Correct Answer: C) 3

3. If \((AB) = 40, (A\beta) = 60, (\alpha B) = 20, (\alpha\beta) = 80\), calculate Yule's Coefficient of Association (\(Q\)).

A) 0.5
B) 0.45
C) 0.6
D) 0.25

Correct Answer: B) 0.45

Calculation: \(Q = \frac{40(80) - 60(20)}{40(80) + 60(20)} = \frac{3200 - 1200}{3200 + 1200} = \frac{2000}{4400} \approx 0.45\)

📝 Subjective Qs

1. Following is the data of marks of 100 students. Calculate Bowley's coefficient of skewness.

Marks0-1010-2020-3030-4040-50
No. of Students1025401510

Step 1: Find Cumulative Frequencies (cf)

10, 35, 75, 90, 100

Step 2: Find Quartiles

\(Q_1 \text{ class: } N/4 = 25^{th} \text{ item} \implies 10-20\)

\(Q_1 = 10 + \frac{25-10}{25} \times 10 = 10 + 6 = 16\)

\(Median \text{ class: } N/2 = 50^{th} \text{ item} \implies 20-30\)

\(Median = 20 + \frac{50-35}{40} \times 10 = 20 + 3.75 = 23.75\)

\(Q_3 \text{ class: } 3N/4 = 75^{th} \text{ item} \implies 20-30\)

\(Q_3 = 30 \text{ (exact upper limit of cf=75 class)}\)

Step 3: Calculate \(S_B\)

\[ \begin{aligned} S_B &= \frac{30 + 16 - 2(23.75)}{30 - 16} \\ &= \frac{46 - 47.5}{14} = \frac{-1.5}{14} \approx -0.107 \end{aligned} \]

The distribution is slightly negatively skewed.
