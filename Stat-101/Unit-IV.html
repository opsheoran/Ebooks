4.1 Skewness: Introduction & Interpretation

4.1 Skewness: परिचय और Interpretation

Measures of central tendency tell us about the concentration of values, and dispersion measures tell us the spread. But neither tells us about the symmetry of the distribution. Skewness fills this gap — it measures the lack of symmetry or the degree to which a distribution departs from symmetry.

Central tendency के measures values के concentration के बारे में बताते हैं, और dispersion spread के बारे में। लेकिन दोनों distribution की symmetry के बारे में नहीं बताते। Skewness यह काम करता है — यह symmetry की कमी या distribution के symmetry से कितना दूर है, उसे मापता है।

📌 Key Idea — When is a Distribution Symmetric? A distribution is symmetric if it is identical on both sides of its centre. If you fold the histogram along the mean, both halves match perfectly. Any deviation from this perfect fold is called skewness.
📌 मुख्य बात — Distribution Symmetric कब होता है? एक distribution symmetric होता है जब वो अपने centre के दोनों sides पर identical होता है। अगर आप histogram को mean के साथ fold करें, तो दोनों halves perfectly match करते हैं। इस perfect fold से कोई भी deviation skewness कहलाता है।

Types of Skewness

Skewness के प्रकार

  • Symmetrical (Zero Skewness): Left and right tails are equal; the curve is bell-shaped. Mean = Median = Mode.
  • Positively Skewed (Right-skewed): Right tail is longer; extreme high values pull the mean upward. Mean > Median > Mode.
  • Negatively Skewed (Left-skewed): Left tail is longer; extreme low values pull the mean downward. Mean < Median < Mode.
  • Symmetrical (Zero Skewness): Left और right tails बराबर होती हैं; curve bell-shaped होता है। Mean = Median = Mode.
  • Positively Skewed (Right-skewed): Right tail लंबी होती है; अत्यधिक ऊँचे values mean को ऊपर खींचते हैं। Mean > Median > Mode.
  • Negatively Skewed (Left-skewed): Left tail लंबी होती है; अत्यधिक कम values mean को नीचे खींचते हैं। Mean < Median < Mode.

4.2 Coefficients of Skewness

4.2 Skewness के Coefficients

Absolute skewness depends on the unit of measurement. To compare different distributions, we use relative (pure number) coefficients.

Absolute skewness measurement की unit पर depend करता है। अलग-अलग distributions compare करने के लिए relative (pure number) coefficients use करते हैं।

1. Karl Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_k\))

1. Karl Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_k\))

\[ S_k = \frac{\text{Mean} - \text{Mode}}{\sigma} \]

When Mode is ill-defined (using empirical relation Mode = 3 Median − 2 Mean):

जब Mode ill-defined हो (empirical relation: Mode = 3 Median − 2 Mean):

\[ S_k = \frac{3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median})}{\sigma} \]

Range: Typically −3 to +3.  |  \(S_k = 0\) → symmetric; \(S_k > 0\) → positively skewed; \(S_k < 0\) → negatively skewed.

Range: आमतौर पर −3 से +3।  |  \(S_k = 0\) → symmetric; \(S_k > 0\) → positively skewed; \(S_k < 0\) → negatively skewed।

2. Bowley's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_Q\))

2. Bowley's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_Q\))

Based on quartiles; ideal for open-ended distributions where extreme values are not known. Always lies between −1 and +1.

Quartiles पर आधारित; open-ended distributions के लिए ideal जहाँ extreme values नहीं पता। हमेशा −1 और +1 के बीच।

\[ S_Q = \frac{Q_3 + Q_1 - 2Q_2}{Q_3 - Q_1} \]

3. Kelly's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_P\))

3. Kelly's Coefficient of Skewness (\(S_P\))

Uses the 10th, 50th, and 90th percentiles (or D₁, D₅, D₉). Ignores extreme 10% on each side.

10th, 50th, और 90th percentiles (या D₁, D₅, D₉) का use करता है। दोनों sides के extreme 10% को ignore करता है।

\[ S_P = \frac{P_{90} + P_{10} - 2P_{50}}{P_{90} - P_{10}} = \frac{D_9 + D_1 - 2D_5}{D_9 - D_1} \]

4.3 Moments: Raw & Central

4.3 Moments: Raw और Central

Moments are summary statistics that capture specific properties of a distribution. They form the basis for calculating coefficients of skewness and kurtosis.

Moments summary statistics हैं जो distribution के specific properties को capture करते हैं। ये skewness और kurtosis के coefficients calculate करने का आधार हैं।

Raw Moments (Moments about Origin)

Raw Moments (Origin के बारे में Moments)

The \(r^{th}\) raw moment is calculated about a fixed value \(A\) (usually 0 or the assumed mean):

\(r^{th}\) raw moment एक fixed value \(A\) (आमतौर पर 0 या assumed mean) के बारे में calculate किया जाता है:

\[\mu'_r = \frac{\sum f(x - A)^r}{N} \quad \text{(for grouped data)}\] \[\mu'_r = \frac{\sum (x - A)^r}{N} \quad \text{(for ungrouped data)}\]

When \(A = 0\): \(\mu'_1 = \bar{x}\) (the arithmetic mean).

जब \(A = 0\): \(\mu'_1 = \bar{x}\) (arithmetic mean)।

Central Moments (Moments about Mean)

Central Moments (Mean के बारे में Moments)

Central moments are calculated about the arithmetic mean \(\bar{x}\):

Central moments arithmetic mean \(\bar{x}\) के बारे में calculate किए जाते हैं:

\[\mu_r = \frac{\sum f(x - \bar{x})^r}{N}\]
Key Properties of Central Moments:
• \(\mu_1 = 0\) always (first central moment is always zero).
• \(\mu_2 = \sigma^2\) (second central moment equals variance).
• \(\mu_3\) measures asymmetry (used in \(\beta_1\), skewness).
• \(\mu_4\) measures peakedness (used in \(\beta_2\), kurtosis).
Central Moments के Key Properties:
• \(\mu_1 = 0\) हमेशा (first central moment हमेशा zero होता है)।
• \(\mu_2 = \sigma^2\) (second central moment variance के बराबर होता है)।
• \(\mu_3\) asymmetry मापता है (skewness \(\beta_1\) में use)।
• \(\mu_4\) peakedness मापता है (kurtosis \(\beta_2\) में use)।

Relationship Between Raw and Central Moments

Raw और Central Moments के बीच Relationship

\[\mu_1 = 0\] \[\mu_2 = \mu'_2 - (\mu'_1)^2\] \[\mu_3 = \mu'_3 - 3\mu'_2 \mu'_1 + 2(\mu'_1)^3\] \[\mu_4 = \mu'_4 - 4\mu'_3 \mu'_1 + 6\mu'_2 (\mu'_1)^2 - 3(\mu'_1)^4\]

Sheppard's Correction for Grouped Data

Grouped Data के लिए Sheppard's Correction

When data is grouped into class intervals of width \(h\), grouping introduces errors in moments. Sheppard's corrections adjust for this:

जब data को width \(h\) के class intervals में group किया जाता है, तो moments में errors आती हैं। Sheppard's corrections इसे adjust करते हैं:

\[\mu_2^{\text{(corrected)}} = \mu_2 - \frac{h^2}{12}\] \[\mu_4^{\text{(corrected)}} = \mu_4 - \frac{h^2}{2}\mu_2 + \frac{7h^4}{240}\]

(\(\mu_1\) and \(\mu_3\) need no correction.)

(\(\mu_1\) और \(\mu_3\) को कोई correction नहीं चाहिए।)

4.4 Kurtosis: Concept & Coefficient

4.4 Kurtosis: Concept और Coefficient

Kurtosis measures the degree of peakedness (or flatness) of a frequency curve relative to the standard normal curve. It tells us about the concentration of values in the tails versus the centre of the distribution.

Kurtosis standard normal curve के मुक़ाबले frequency curve के peakedness की degree (या flatness) को मापता है। यह हमें distribution के centre बनाम tails में values के concentration के बारे में बताता है।

Types of Kurtosis

Kurtosis के प्रकार

Mesokurtic (\(\beta_2 = 3\))
Normal, bell-shaped curve. Neither too peaked nor too flat. Standard reference point. Example: heights of adult males in a large population. Normal, bell-shaped curve। न बहुत peaked, न बहुत flat। Standard reference point। उदाहरण: large population में वयस्क पुरुषों की ऊँचाई।
Leptokurtic (\(\beta_2 > 3\))
More peaked than normal; heavy/fat tails; most values cluster tightly around mean with more extreme values. Example: financial returns, rainfall events. Normal से ज़्यादा peaked; heavy/fat tails; अधिकतर values mean के आस-पास cluster करती हैं, extreme values ज़्यादा। उदाहरण: financial returns, rainfall events।
Platykurtic (\(\beta_2 < 3\))
Flatter than normal; thin tails; values spread more uniformly. Example: uniform distribution of seeds in a packet, exam scores when all students equally prepared. Normal से ज़्यादा flat; thin tails; values ज़्यादा uniformly spread। उदाहरण: एक packet में seeds का uniform distribution, तब exam scores जब सभी छात्र equally prepared हों।

4.9 Yule's Coefficient of Association (\(Q\)) & Colligation (\(Y\))

4.9 Yule's Coefficient of Association (\(Q\)) और Colligation (\(Y\))

G.U. Yule (1900) developed two coefficients to measure the degree and direction of association between two attributes.

G.U. Yule (1900) ने दो attributes के बीच association की degree और direction मापने के लिए दो coefficients विकसित किए।

1. Yule's Coefficient of Association (\(Q\))

1. Yule's Coefficient of Association (\(Q\))

\[ Q = \frac{(AB)(\alpha\beta) - (A\beta)(\alpha B)}{(AB)(\alpha\beta) + (A\beta)(\alpha B)} \]

The two products \((AB)(\alpha\beta)\) and \((A\beta)(\alpha B)\) are called the cross products of the 2×2 contingency table.

दो products \((AB)(\alpha\beta)\) और \((A\beta)(\alpha B)\) को 2×2 contingency table के cross products कहते हैं।

Properties of Q:  −1 ≤ Q ≤ +1  |  Q = 0 → independence  |  Q = +1 → complete positive association (one cell = 0)  |  Q = −1 → complete negative association (other cell = 0)
Q के Properties:  −1 ≤ Q ≤ +1  |  Q = 0 → independence  |  Q = +1 → complete positive association (एक cell = 0)  |  Q = −1 → complete negative association (दूसरा cell = 0)

2. Yule's Coefficient of Colligation (\(Y\))

2. Yule's Coefficient of Colligation (\(Y\))

\[ Y = \frac{\sqrt{(AB)(\alpha\beta)} - \sqrt{(A\beta)(\alpha B)}}{\sqrt{(AB)(\alpha\beta)} + \sqrt{(A\beta)(\alpha B)}} \]

Relationship Between Q and Y

Q और Y के बीच Relationship

\[ Q = \frac{2Y}{1 + Y^2} \qquad Y = \frac{1 - \sqrt{1-Q^2}}{Q} \quad (Q \ne 0) \]

Also: \(|Q| \ge |Y|\) always (i.e., Q is numerically larger than Y).

साथ ही: \(|Q| \ge |Y|\) हमेशा (यानी Q numerically Y से बड़ा होता है)।

Q1. Prove that \(\beta_2 \ge \beta_1 + 1\) for any frequency distribution.

Solution: This is a standard property of moments. It can be proved using the variance of a quadratic function or the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. The variance of \( (X - \bar{x})^2 + c(X - \bar{x}) \) is always non-negative, which ultimately reduces to:

\[ \begin{aligned} \mu_4 \mu_2 - \mu_3^2 - \mu_2^3 &\ge 0 \\ \frac{\mu_4 \mu_2}{\mu_2^3} - \frac{\mu_3^2}{\mu_2^3} - \frac{\mu_2^3}{\mu_2^3} &\ge 0 \\ \beta_2 - \beta_1 - 1 &\ge 0 \\ \beta_2 &\ge \beta_1 + 1 \end{aligned} \]

∎

Q2. Prove the relationship between Yule's Coefficient of Association (Q) and Coefficient of Colligation (Y): \(Q = \frac{2Y}{1+Y^2}\).

Solution: Let \(p = \sqrt{(AB)(\alpha\beta)}\) and \(q = \sqrt{(A\beta)(\alpha B)}\). Then \(Y = \frac{p-q}{p+q}\) and \(Q = \frac{p^2-q^2}{p^2+q^2}\). Expanding \(\frac{2Y}{1+Y^2}\) by substituting Y:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{2Y}{1+Y^2} &= \frac{2\left(\frac{p-q}{p+q}\right)}{1+\left(\frac{p-q}{p+q}\right)^2} \\ &= \frac{\frac{2(p-q)}{p+q}}{\frac{(p+q)^2+(p-q)^2}{(p+q)^2}} \\ &= \frac{2(p-q)(p+q)}{(p+q)^2+(p-q)^2} \\ &= \frac{2(p^2-q^2)}{2(p^2+q^2)} \\ &= Q \end{aligned} \]

∎

The distribution is positively skewed.

Solution: Consistency requires \(\max(0, (A)+(B)-N) \le (AB) \le \min((A), (B))\).

Substituting \(A = N/2\) and \(B = N/2\):

\[ \begin{aligned} \max\left(0, \frac{N}{2}+\frac{N}{2}-N\right) &\le (AB) \le \min\left(\frac{N}{2}, \frac{N}{2}\right) \\ \max(0, 0) &\le (AB) \le \frac{N}{2} \\ 0 &\le (AB) \le \frac{N}{2} \end{aligned} \]

∎

📝 Subjective Questions (Data Based)

Q1. Prove करें कि किसी भी frequency distribution के लिए \(\beta_2 \ge \beta_1 + 1\) होता है।

Solution: यह moments की एक standard property है जिसे Cauchy-Schwarz inequality या quadratic function के variance द्वारा prove किया जा सकता है। \( (X - \bar{x})^2 + c(X - \bar{x}) \) का variance हमेशा non-negative होता है, जो ultimately इस प्रकार है:

\[ \begin{aligned} \mu_4 \mu_2 - \mu_3^2 - \mu_2^3 &\ge 0 \\ \frac{\mu_4 \mu_2}{\mu_2^3} - \frac{\mu_3^2}{\mu_2^3} - \frac{\mu_2^3}{\mu_2^3} &\ge 0 \\ \beta_2 - \beta_1 - 1 &\ge 0 \\ \beta_2 &\ge \beta_1 + 1 \end{aligned} \]

∎

Q2. Yule's Q और Y के बीच relationship prove करें: \(Q = \frac{2Y}{1+Y^2}\)।

Solution: मान लें \(p = \sqrt{(AB)(\alpha\beta)}\) और \(q = \sqrt{(A\beta)(\alpha B)}\)। तब \(Y = \frac{p-q}{p+q}\) और \(Q = \frac{p^2-q^2}{p^2+q^2}\)। \(\frac{2Y}{1+Y^2}\) को Y substitute करके expand करें:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{2Y}{1+Y^2} &= \frac{2\left(\frac{p-q}{p+q}\right)}{1+\left(\frac{p-q}{p+q}\right)^2} \\ &= \frac{\frac{2(p-q)}{p+q}}{\frac{(p+q)^2+(p-q)^2}{(p+q)^2}} \\ &= \frac{2(p-q)(p+q)}{(p+q)^2+(p-q)^2} \\ &= \frac{2(p^2-q^2)}{2(p^2+q^2)} \\ &= Q \end{aligned} \]

∎

Q3. Mean = 50, Median = 48, σ = 10. Karl Pearson's coefficient of skewness निकालें।

Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} S_k &= \frac{3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median})}{\sigma} \\ &= \frac{3(50 - 48)}{10} \\ &= \frac{6}{10} \\ &= +0.6 \end{aligned} \]

Distribution positively skewed है।

Q8. N=400, (A)=250, (B)=150, (AB)=100. Yule's Q निकालें।

Solution: पहले ultimate frequencies निकालें:

\[ \begin{aligned} (A\beta) &= 250 - 100 = 150 \\ (\alpha B) &= 150 - 100 = 50 \\ (\alpha\beta) &= 400 - 250 - 150 + 100 = 100 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} Q &= \frac{(AB)(\alpha\beta) - (A\beta)(\alpha B)}{(AB)(\alpha\beta) + (A\beta)(\alpha B)} \\ &= \frac{100(100) - 150(50)}{100(100) + 150(50)} \\ &= \frac{10000 - 7500}{10000 + 7500} \\ &= +0.143 \end{aligned} \]
Q9. Q8 के data से Y निकालें और verify करें।

Solution: मान लें \(p = \sqrt{(AB)(\alpha\beta)}\) और \(q = \sqrt{(A\beta)(\alpha B)}\):

\[ \begin{aligned} p &= \sqrt{10000} = 100 \\ q &= \sqrt{7500} \approx 86.60 \\ Y &= \frac{p-q}{p+q} = \frac{100 - 86.60}{100 + 86.60} \approx 0.0718 \end{aligned} \]

Verification:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{2Y}{1 + Y^2} &= \frac{2(0.0718)}{1 + (0.0718)^2} \\ &\approx \frac{0.1436}{1.00515} \\ &\approx 0.143 \\ &= Q \quad \text{✓} \end{aligned} \]
