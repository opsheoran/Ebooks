4.1 Introduction to Moments

In statistics, the term 'Moment' is used to describe the characteristics of a frequency distribution. Just as arithmetic mean and standard deviation are used to measure central tendency and dispersion, moments provide a systematic method to analyze the shape (Symmetry and Peakedness) of a distribution. The name 'Moment' is borrowed from Physics, where it refers to the product of a force and its distance from a point.

Statistics में, 'Moment' शब्द का उपयोग frequency distribution की विशेषताओं (characteristics) को describe करने के लिए किया जाता है। जिस तरह arithmetic mean और standard deviation का उपयोग central tendency और dispersion को मापने के लिए किया जाता है, moments distribution के shape (Symmetry और Peakedness) का विश्लेषण करने के लिए एक व्यवस्थित (systematic) तरीका प्रदान करते हैं। 'Moment' नाम Physics से लिया गया है, जहाँ यह एक force और एक point से उसकी दूरी के गुणनफल (product) को संदर्भित करता है।

Basic Definition

The \(r^{th}\) moment of a variable \(X\) about any arbitrary point \(A\) is the arithmetic mean of the \(r^{th}\) powers of the deviations of the observations from \(A\). It is denoted by \(\mu'_r\).

किसी भी arbitrary point \(A\) के परितः (about) variable \(X\) का \(r^{th}\) moment, \(A\) से observations के deviations की \(r^{th}\) powers का arithmetic mean होता है। इसे \(\mu'_r\) द्वारा दर्शाया जाता है।

\[ \mu'_r = \frac{\sum f_i (X_i - A)^r}{N} \]

4.2 Central Moments

When deviations are taken from the arithmetic mean (\(\bar{X}\)), the moments are called Central Moments or Moments about the Mean. These moments are purely independent of the origin of measurement and describe the internal structure of the distribution.

जब deviations arithmetic mean (\(\bar{X}\)) से लिए जाते हैं, तो moments को Central Moments या Mean के परितः moments कहा जाता है। ये moments measurement के origin से पूरी तरह स्वतंत्र होते हैं और distribution के internal structure को describe करते हैं।

\[ \mu_r = \frac{\sum f_i (X_i - \bar{X})^r}{N} \]

Properties of Central Moments:

  • \(\mu_0 = 1\): The zero-order central moment is always 1.
  • \(\mu_1 = 0\): The first central moment is always zero (since the sum of deviations from the mean is zero).
  • \(\mu_2 = \sigma^2\): The second central moment is the Variance.
  • \(\mu_3\): Used to measure Skewness.
  • \(\mu_4\): Used to measure Kurtosis.

4.3 Raw Moments

Raw Moments (or Moments about any point \(A\)) are calculated when the arithmetic mean is not a whole number or when we want to calculate moments about zero. Moments about zero are specifically called Moments about Origin.

Raw Moments (या किसी भी point \(A\) के परितः moments) तब calculate किए जाते हैं जब arithmetic mean एक whole number नहीं होता है या जब हम zero के परितः moments calculate करना चाहते हैं। zero के परितः moments को विशेष रूप से Moments about Origin कहा जाता है।

\[ \mu'_r = \frac{\sum f_i d_i^r}{N} \quad \text{where } d_i = X_i - A \]

Example: Moments about Origin

When \(A = 0\), the raw moments (\(\mu'_r\)) represent the arithmetic mean of the \(r^{th}\) power of the observations. For example, \(\mu'_1 = \frac{\sum f_i X_i}{N}\), which is the Arithmetic Mean.

जब \(A = 0\) होता है, तो raw moments (\(\mu'_r\)) observations की \(r^{th}\) power के arithmetic mean को represent करते हैं। उदाहरण के लिए, \(\mu'_1 = \frac{\sum f_i X_i}{N}\), जो कि Arithmetic Mean है।

4.4 Relationship Between Central & Raw Moments

It is often easier to calculate moments about an arbitrary point \(A\) and then convert them to central moments. The general relationship between central moments (\(\mu\)) and raw moments (\(\mu'\)) is given by the binomial expansion:

अक्सर एक arbitrary point \(A\) के परितः moments calculate करना और फिर उन्हें central moments में बदलना आसान होता है। central moments (\(\mu\)) और raw moments (\(\mu'\)) के बीच सामान्य संबंध binomial expansion द्वारा दिया जाता है:

First Four Central Moments:

  1. \(\mu_1 = 0\)
  2. \(\mu_2 = \mu'_2 - (\mu'_1)^2\)
  3. \(\mu_3 = \mu'_3 - 3\mu'_2\mu'_1 + 2(\mu'_1)^3\)
  4. \(\mu_4 = \mu'_4 - 4\mu'_3\mu'_1 + 6\mu'_2(\mu'_1)^2 - 3(\mu'_1)^4\)

Note: \(\mu'_1\) is the deviation of the mean from the arbitrary point \(A\), i.e., \(\mu'_1 = \bar{X} - A\).

Note: \(\mu'_1\) arbitrary point \(A\) से mean का deviation है, यानी, \(\mu'_1 = \bar{X} - A\).

4.5 Sheppard's Correction for Moments

When calculating moments for grouped frequency distributions, we assume that the frequencies are concentrated at the mid-points of the classes. This assumption introduces a grouping error. Sheppard's Correction is applied to adjust these errors, provided the frequency distribution is continuous and tails off to zero at both ends.

Grouped frequency distributions के लिए moments calculate करते समय, हम मान लेते हैं कि frequencies classes के mid-points पर केंद्रित (concentrated) हैं। यह धारणा एक grouping error पैदा करती है। इन errors को adjust करने के लिए Sheppard's Correction लागू किया जाता है, बशर्ते frequency distribution continuous हो और दोनों सिरों पर zero तक कम हो जाए।

Let \(h\) be the class interval width:

  • Corrected \(\mu_2 = \mu_2(\text{obs}) - \frac{h^2}{12}\)
  • Corrected \(\mu_3 = \mu_3(\text{obs})\) (No correction for odd moments)
  • Corrected \(\mu_4 = \mu_4(\text{obs}) - \frac{h^2}{2}\mu_2(\text{obs}) + \frac{7h^4}{240}\)

4.6 Skewness

Skewness is the measure of the asymmetry of a frequency distribution. A distribution is said to be skewed if it is not symmetrical. In a symmetrical distribution, Mean, Median, and Mode are all equal and coincide at the center.

Skewness frequency distribution की asymmetry (असममिति) का माप है। एक distribution को skewed कहा जाता है यदि वह symmetrical नहीं है। एक symmetrical distribution में, Mean, Median और Mode सभी समान होते हैं और केंद्र में एक साथ होते हैं।

Types of Skewness:

  1. Positive Skewness: Long tail on the right side. (Mean > Median > Mode)
  2. Negative Skewness: Long tail on the left side. (Mode > Median > Mean)

Measures of Skewness

The two most commonly used measures are based on Mean/Mode/SD (Karl Pearson) and Quartiles (Bowley).

सबसे अधिक उपयोग किए जाने वाले दो माप Mean/Mode/SD (Karl Pearson) और Quartiles (Bowley) पर आधारित हैं।

1. Karl Pearson's Coefficient (\(S_k\)):

\[ S_k = \frac{\text{Mean} - \text{Mode}}{\sigma} \]

2. Bowley's Coefficient (\(S_b\)):

\[ S_b = \frac{Q_3 + Q_1 - 2\text{Md}}{Q_3 - Q_1} \]

3. Based on Moments (\(\beta_1\)):

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{\mu_3^2}{\mu_2^3} \quad \text{and} \quad \gamma_1 = \sqrt{\beta_1} = \frac{\mu_3}{\sigma^3} \]

Example: Skewness Analysis

Consider a distribution of farmers' income in Haryana. If the majority of farmers have low income but a few have exceptionally high income, the tail will extend to the right, indicating Positive Skewness.

हरियाणा में किसानों की आय (income) के distribution पर विचार करें। यदि अधिकांश किसानों की आय कम है लेकिन कुछ की आय बहुत अधिक है, तो tail दाईं (right) ओर बढ़ जाएगी, जो Positive Skewness का संकेत देती है।

4.7 Kurtosis

While skewness measures the asymmetry, Kurtosis measures the 'peakedness' or 'flatness' of the distribution curve relative to the Normal Curve. The word is derived from the Greek 'Kurtos' meaning 'bulging' or 'curved'.

जहाँ skewness asymmetry को मापती है, वहीं Kurtosis Normal Curve के सापेक्ष distribution curve की 'peakedness' (नुकीलापन) या 'flatness' (चपटापन) को मापती है। यह शब्द Greek के 'Kurtos' से लिया गया है जिसका अर्थ है 'bulging' या 'curved'।

Three Types of Curves:

  • Leptokurtic: Very peaked and narrow top. (\(\beta_2 > 3\))
  • Mesokurtic: Normal peakedness. (\(\beta_2 = 3\))
  • Platykurtic: Flat-topped and wide. (\(\beta_2 < 3\))

Measure of Kurtosis (\(\beta_2\))

\[ \beta_2 = \frac{\mu_4}{\mu_2^2} \quad \text{and} \quad \gamma_2 = \beta_2 - 3 \]

If \(\gamma_2 > 0\), it's Leptokurtic. If \(\gamma_2 < 0\), it's Platykurtic. If \(\gamma_2 = 0\), it's Mesokurtic.

4.8 Exercises

1. The value of the first central moment (\(\mu_1\)) is always:

A) One (1)
B) Zero (0)
C) Variance
D) Standard Deviation

Correct Answer: B) Zero (0)

The sum of deviations from the arithmetic mean is always zero, hence \(\mu_1 = 0\).

2. For a Mesokurtic distribution, the value of \(\beta_2\) is:

A) 0
B) < 3
C) 3
D) > 3

Correct Answer: C) 3

3. If Mean = 45, Median = 48, and \(\sigma\) = 10, the Karl Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness is:

A) 0.9
B) -0.9
C) 0.3
D) -0.3

Correct Answer: B) -0.9

Calculation: \(S_k = \frac{3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median})}{\sigma} = \frac{3(45 - 48)}{10} = \frac{-9}{10} = -0.9\).

Numerical Exercise:

For a distribution, the first four moments about the point \(A = 5\) are 2, 20, 40, and 50. Calculate the central moments (\(\mu_2, \mu_3, \mu_4\)).

Given: \(\mu'_1=2, \mu'_2=20, \mu'_3=40, \mu'_4=50\)

1. \(\mu_2 = \mu'_2 - (\mu'_1)^2 = 20 - (2)^2 = 16\)

2. \(\mu_3 = \mu'_3 - 3\mu'_2\mu'_1 + 2(\mu'_1)^3 = 40 - 3(20)(2) + 2(2)^3 = 40 - 120 + 16 = -64\)

3. \(\mu_4 = \mu'_4 - 4\mu'_3\mu'_1 + 6\mu'_2(\mu'_1)^2 - 3(\mu'_1)^4 = 50 - 4(40)(2) + 6(20)(4) - 3(16) = 50 - 320 + 480 - 48 = 162\)
