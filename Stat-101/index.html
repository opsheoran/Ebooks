About This Course

STAT-M-101: Statistical Methods-I is a core minor (vocational) course offered in Semester I carrying 2 credit hours. It provides a comprehensive introduction to the fundamental concepts of statistics, its origins, and its vast applications.

The course systematically guides students through the entire data lifecycle. It begins with the collection and scrutiny of data (Unit I), moves into clear presentation and measuring the 'center' (Unit II), explores the 'spread' and statistical moments (Unit III), and finally concludes with advanced descriptors like skewness, kurtosis, and the theory of attributes for categorical data (Unit IV).

This textbook is carefully designed to build your empirical reasoning. Each unit integrates theoretical foundations with practical exercises, ensuring you can apply these methods accurately to real-world datasets.

Preface

Data is the new foundation of the modern world. However, raw data is simply a chaotic collection of numbers until we apply statistical methods to extract meaning, patterns, and actionable insights from it. This web-based textbook on Statistical Methods-I has been crafted specifically for the students of STAT-M-101 at CCS Haryana Agricultural University, Hisar, perfectly aligning with the Semester I syllabus.

The philosophy guiding this material is accessibility without compromising accuracy. Statistics can initially appear daunting due to its formulas, but every formula is simply a logical tool designed to answer a specific question about the world. By introducing concepts chronologically—from collecting data to advanced distribution measures—this book aims to make that logic transparent.

Statistical thinking will one day be as necessary for efficient citizenship as the ability to read and write.

— H.G. Wells

Scope and Organisation

The course is systematically divided into four progressive units. Unit I addresses the very beginning of any study: what statistics is, what data is, and how we properly collect and scrutinize it to ensure our foundation is solid.

Unit II focuses on visual and numerical summaries. It covers the presentation of data and the foundational Measures of Central Tendency (Mean, Median, Mode), teaching us how to find the 'typical' value of a dataset.

Unit III delves deeper by exploring how data spreads out around that center. It introduces Measures of Dispersion (variance, standard deviation) and statistical Moments, providing a much richer picture of the data's behavior.

Unit IV concludes the course by examining the shape of data distributions through Skewness and Kurtosis, and introduces the Theory of Attributes, which allows us to mathematically analyze categorical data and determine associations between qualitative traits.

Pedagogical Features

This digital textbook includes detailed theoretical explanations, illustrative tables, fully worked examples, and comprehensive exercise sets. The exercises are formatted as interactive Multiple-Choice, Fill-in-the-blanks, True/False, and Subjective questions, complete with toggleable solutions to encourage active learning and self-assessment.

Mastering statistics requires practice. Students are highly encouraged to work through the data-based subjective questions by hand before checking the provided solutions.

Course Syllabus

The following table presents the detailed unit-wise syllabus for STAT-M-101: Statistical Methods-I as prescribed for Semester I.

Unit Title Topics Covered
Unit I Introduction & Data Collection Origin, development, definition, scope, uses, limitations. Types of Data. Collection and Scrutiny of Data.
Unit II Presentation & Central Tendency Presentation of Data. Measures of Central Tendency and Location. Partition Values.

A Note from the Author

Dear Students,

Welcome to Statistical Methods-I (STAT-M-101). For many of you, this might be your first formal introduction to the rigorous discipline of statistics. Often, students perceive statistics merely as a branch of mathematics concerned with endless calculations. I encourage you to view it differently: statistics is a powerful lens through which we can understand the real world.

Whether you are analyzing crop yields, studying economic trends, or making policy decisions, statistical methods provide the framework to separate meaningful signals from random noise. The concepts you learn in this foundational course—from basic data collection to understanding variation—will remain relevant throughout your academic and professional careers.

Data are just summaries of thousands of stories—tell a few of those stories to help make the data meaningful.

— Chip and Dan Heath

How to Succeed in this Course

1. Understand the 'Why': Before memorizing a formula for variance or skewness, make sure you understand why we need it. What real-world question does it answer?

2. Focus on Data Collection: Pay close attention to Unit I. The most sophisticated analysis cannot rescue poorly collected data. Understanding how to properly gather and scrutinize data is perhaps the most important skill you will learn.

3. Practice Relentlessly: Use the interactive exercises at the end of each unit. Attempt the problems independently before checking the solutions. It is through making mistakes and correcting them that true learning occurs.

4. Connect to Reality: Whenever possible, try to apply these statistical concepts to news articles, research papers, or daily observations.

Do not hesitate to ask questions. If a concept seems abstract or confusing, reach out. The goal is complete comprehension, and I am here to help you achieve it.

I wish you a rewarding and enlightening semester ahead.
