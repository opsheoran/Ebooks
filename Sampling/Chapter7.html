7.1 Introduction

"At thirty man suspects himself a fool;
Knows it at forty, and reforms his plan:
At fifty chides his infamous delay,
Pushes his prudent purpose to resolve,
In all the magnanimity of thought
Resolves, and re-resolves: then dies the same."
-- J. Young

Like ratio estimators, linear regression estimators also make use of auxiliary information for increasing precision. It was seen that the ratio estimator provides a precise estimate of the population mean if regression is linear and the line passes through the origin. When regression is linear and the line does not go through the origin, it is better to use estimators based on linear regression. In other words, if the study variate (\( y \)) is approximately a constant and a multiple of the auxiliary variate, it is more precise to estimate the population mean or total by fitting a linear regression. Such an estimator is called a regression estimator. We shall now discuss the main characteristics of such estimators and compare them with the mean per unit estimator and ratio estimator.

Ratio estimators की तरह, linear regression estimators भी precision बढ़ाने के लिए auxiliary information का उपयोग करते हैं। हमने देखा था कि ratio estimator तब सटीक होता है जब regression linear हो और line origin (मूल बिंदु) से गुजरे। लेकिन जब regression linear हो और line origin से न गुजरे, तो linear regression आधारित estimators बेहतर होते हैं। दूसरे शब्दों में, अगर study variable (\( y \)) auxiliary variable का लगभग linear function हो, तो linear regression फिट करके population parameters का अनुमान लगाना ज़्यादा सटीक होता है। ऐसे estimator को regression estimator कहा जाता है। हम इसके मुख्य characteristics और अन्य estimators से तुलना पर चर्चा करेंगे।

7.2 Difference Estimator

Let \( y \) and \( x \) be correlated characteristics. We wish to estimate \( Y \). If, from a simple random sample, we obtain the unbiased estimators \( \bar{y} \) and \( \bar{x} \) of \( Y \) and \( X \), respectively, then we can improve upon the estimator \( \bar{y} \) by introducing a difference function. Thus, a simple difference estimator is defined by

$$ \bar{y}_D = \bar{y} + (\bar{X} - \bar{x}) \quad (7.2.1) $$

It is assumed that there is a unit change in \( y \) when a unit change is made in \( x \), \( x \) and \( y \) having equal variances. This assumption will not be valid if the relationship is of the type \( y = cx + k \), where \( c \) and \( k \) are constants. In this situation, a more generalized difference estimator is defined as

$$ \bar{y}_D = \bar{y} + c(\bar{X} - \bar{x}) \quad (7.2.2) $$

where \( c \) and \( \bar{X} \) are known quantities.

In simple random sampling, the difference estimator and its variance are discussed in the following theorems.

Theorem 7.2.1

In simple random sampling, wor, the difference estimator \( \bar{y}_D \) is unbiased and its sampling variance is given by

$$ V(\bar{y}_D) = V(\bar{y}) + c^2V(\bar{x}) - 2c\text{ Cov}(\bar{y},\bar{x}) $$ $$ = V(\bar{y})(1 + \alpha^2 - 2\alpha\rho) \quad (7.2.3) $$

where \( \rho \) is the correlation coefficient between \( y \) and \( x \), and \( \alpha = cS_x/S_y \).

The proof is simple, and its development is left as an exercise. From this result, it may be noted that \( \bar{y}_D \) has smaller variance than \( \bar{y} \) if \( \rho > \alpha/2 \).

Corollary

Show that \( V(\bar{y}_D) \) will have its minimum value when

$$ c = \text{Cov}(\bar{y},\bar{x})/V(\bar{x}) \quad (7.2.4) $$

This proves that the optimum value to be given to \( c \) is \( \beta \), the value of the regression coefficient of \( y \) on \( x \). In this case, \( \alpha = \rho \) and the minimum value of variance is \( V(\bar{y})(1 - \rho^2) \).

Theorem 7.2.2

In simple random sampling, wor, the sampling variance of \( \bar{y}_D \) is obtained by

$$ V(\bar{y}_D) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}(S_y^2 + c^2S_x^2 - 2\rho cS_yS_x) \quad (7.2.5) $$

where \( c \) is some specified quantity.

Proof:

Since \( c \) is a given constant and \( E(\bar{x} - \bar{X}) = 0 \), therefore, the unbiasedness of \( \bar{y}_D \) is proved. Further, denoting

$$ u_i = y_i - c(x_i - \bar{X}), \quad \bar{u} = \bar{y} - c(\bar{x} - \bar{X}) $$

and \( U = Y - c(X - \bar{X}) = Y \), we have

$$ V(\bar{y}_D) = V(\bar{u}) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}S_u^2 $$

Hence,

$$ S_u^2 = \frac{\sum (u_i - \bar{U})^2}{(N - 1)} = \frac{\sum [(y_i - \bar{Y}) - c(x_i - \bar{X})]^2}{(N - 1)} = S_y^2 + c^2S_x^2 - 2c\rho S_yS_x $$
\( \because \) expanding the square clearly yields the respective variances and the covariance term mathematically scaled by the exact constant \( c \).

Corollary 3

In simple random sampling, wor, an unbiased estimator of \( V(\bar{y}_D) \) is obtained by

$$ v(\bar{y}_D) = v(\bar{y}) + c^2v(\bar{x}) - 2c\text{ cov}(\bar{y},\bar{x}) $$ $$ = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}(s_y^2 + c^2s_x^2 - 2cs_{yx}) \quad (7.2.6) $$

7.2 Difference Estimator (Difference Estimator)

मान लीजिए \( y \) और \( x \) correlated variables हैं। हम \( Y \) का अनुमान लगाना चाहते हैं। अगर simple random sample से \( \bar{y} \) और \( \bar{x} \) (unbiased estimators) प्राप्त हों, तो difference function जोड़कर \( \bar{y} \) को सुधारा जा सकता है। एक simple difference estimator इस प्रकार है:

$$ \bar{y}_D = \bar{y} + (\bar{X} - \bar{x}) \quad (7.2.1) $$

यहाँ माना जाता है कि \( x \) में unit change होने पर \( y \) में भी unit change होता है। अगर relationship \( y = cx + k \) टाइप की हो, तो generalized difference estimator उपयोग किया जाता है:

$$ \bar{y}_D = \bar{y} + c(\bar{X} - \bar{x}) \quad (7.2.2) $$

जहाँ \( c \) और \( \bar{X} \) ज्ञात (known) हैं।

Theorem 7.2.1

Simple random sampling (wor) में \( \bar{y}_D \) unbiased है और इसका sampling variance Eq. (7.2.3) द्वारा दिया जाता है। \( \bar{y}_D \) का variance \( \bar{y} \) से कम होगा यदि \( \rho > \alpha/2 \) हो।

Corollary (उपप्रमेय)

\( V(\bar{y}_D) \) न्यूनतम (minimum) तब होगा जब \( c \) का मान regression coefficient \( \beta \) के बराबर हो। तब minimum variance \( V(\bar{y})(1 - \rho^2) \) होता है।

Theorem 7.2.2

Sampling variance और उसका unbiased estimator Eq. (7.2.5) और (7.2.6) द्वारा दिए गए हैं (अंग्रेजी section देखें)।

7.3 Regression Estimator

While discussing the difference estimator, it was seen that the optimum value to be given to \( c \) is \( \beta \), where \( \beta \) is the regression coefficient of \( y \) on \( x \). Generally, \( \beta \) is not known in advance and its value is estimated from the sample. Suppose \( y_i \) and \( x_i \) are obtained for each unit in the sample, then a least square estimate of \( \beta \) is

$$ b = \frac{\sum (y_i - \bar{y})(x_i - \bar{x})}{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2} $$

Thus, linear regression estimators of the population mean \( \bar{Y} \) and population total \( Y \) are given by

$$ \bar{y}_l = \bar{y} + b(\bar{X} - \bar{x}) $$

and

$$ \widehat{Y}_l = N[\bar{y} + b(\bar{X} - \bar{x})] \quad (7.3.1) $$

7.3.1 Bias of Regression Estimator

The regression estimator is consistent because as \( n \to N \), \( \bar{x} \to \bar{X} \) and \( \bar{y}_l \to \bar{Y} \). However, \( \bar{y}_l \) is biased because \( b \) is estimated from the sample. The bias decreases as sample size increases.

Theorem 7.3.1

In simple random sampling, the bias of \( \bar{y}_l \) is approximated by

$$ B(\bar{y}_l) \cong - \text{Cov}(\bar{x},b) \quad (7.3.2) $$

7.3.2 Sampling Variance of Regression Estimator

For large samples, neglecting second and higher order terms, the variance is derived.

Theorem 7.3.2

In simple random sampling, the large sample variance of the regression estimator is given by

$$ V(\bar{y}_l) = V(\bar{y}) + \beta^2V(\bar{x}) - 2\beta \text{Cov}(\bar{y},\bar{x}) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}S_y^2(1 - \rho^2) \quad (7.3.7) $$

This shows that regression estimation is more precise than simple mean if \( \rho \neq 0 \).

Corollary 2

In large samples, an almost unbiased estimator of \( V(\bar{y}_l) \) is:

$$ v(\bar{y}_l) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n(n - 1)}\left[ \sum (y_i - \bar{y})^2 - \frac{\left( \sum (y_i - \bar{y})(x_i - \bar{x}) \right)^2}{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2} \right] \quad (7.3.8) $$

7.3 Regression Estimator (Regression Estimator)

आमतौर पर \( \beta \) पहले से ज्ञात नहीं होता, इसलिए इसे sample से estimate किया जाता है। Least square estimate \( b \) का उपयोग करके linear regression estimators इस प्रकार हैं:

$$ \bar{y}_l = \bar{y} + b(\bar{X} - \bar{x}) $$

7.3.1 Bias (पूर्वाग्रह)

यह estimator consistent है, लेकिन biased है क्योंकि \( b \) sample पर आधारित है। Bias नगण्य होता है यदि sample size बड़ा हो:

$$ B(\bar{y}_l) \cong - \text{Cov}(\bar{x},b) $$

7.3.2 Sampling Variance (Sampling Variance)

बड़े samples के लिए regression estimator का variance इस प्रकार है:

$$ V(\bar{y}_l) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}S_y^2(1 - \rho^2) \quad (7.3.7) $$

यह दर्शाता है कि अगर \( \rho \neq 0 \) हो, तो regression estimator simple mean से ज़्यादा सटीक है। इसका unbiased sample estimator Eq. (7.3.8) में दिया गया है।

7.4 Comparison with the Mean per unit and Ratio Estimators

For large sample size, we compare variances:

  • \( V(\bar{y}) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}S_y^2 \)
  • \( V(\widehat{Y}_R) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}(S_y^2 - 2R\rho S_yS_x + R^2S_x^2) \)
  • \( V(\bar{y}_l) = \frac{(1 - f)}{n}(1 - \rho^2)S_y^2 \)

Regression estimator is always more efficient than simple mean (unless \( \rho = 0 \)) and ratio estimator (unless the regression line passes through the origin).

7.4 Comparison (तुलना)

बड़े samples के लिए, regression estimator हमेशा simple mean (\( \rho \neq 0 \) होने पर) और ratio estimator (जब तक line origin से न गुजरे) से ज़्यादा efficient होता है।

  • यदि \( \rho \cong \sigma_x/\sigma_y \) हो, तो \( c=1 \) वाला difference estimator अच्छा रहता है।
  • यदि \( \rho \cong C_x/C_y \) हो, तो ratio estimator regression जितना ही सटीक होता है।

7.5 Regression Estimators in Stratified Sampling

Similar to ratio estimators, there are two types:

7.5.1 Separate Regression Estimator

$$ \bar{y}_{ls} = \sum W_m[\bar{y}_m + b_m(\bar{X}_m - \bar{x}_m)] \quad (7.5.1) $$

It is more precise if \( b_m \) varies significantly across strata, but can have larger bias in small samples.

7.5.2 Combined Regression Estimator

$$ \bar{y}_{lc} = \bar{y}_{st} + b_c(\bar{X} - \bar{x}_{st}) \quad (7.5.5) $$

It uses a pooled regression coefficient \( b_c \). It is preferred if \( b_m \) is similar across strata, as it has smaller bias.

7.5 Regression Estimators in Stratified Sampling (Stratified Sampling में Regression Estimators)

इसमें दो प्रकार के estimators होते हैं:

7.5.1 Separate Regression Estimator (अलग प्रतिगमन आकलक)

इसमें हर stratum के लिए अलग \( b_m \) निकाला जाता है। यह तब बेहतर है जब strata के slopes अलग-अलग हों, लेकिन छोटे samples में bias ज़्यादा हो सकता है।

7.5.2 Combined Regression Estimator (संयुक्त प्रतिगमन आकलक)

इसमें पूरी population के लिए एक pooled \( b_c \) निकाला जाता है। अगर सभी strata के slopes लगभग समान हों, तो इसे प्राथमिकता दी जाती है क्योंकि इसका bias कम होता है।

7.6 Multi-variate Regression Estimator

When data on more than one auxiliary variate are available:

$$ \bar{y}_{st_i} = \bar{y} + \sum_{i = 1}^k \beta_i(\bar{X}_i - \bar{x}_i) \quad (7.6.1) $$

It is always better to use multiple auxiliary variates if they are not perfectly correlated with each other.

7.6 Multi-variate Regression Estimator (बहुभिन्नरूपी प्रतिगमन आकलक)

जब एक से ज़्यादा auxiliary variables उपलब्ध हों, तब multivariate regression estimator (Eq. 7.6.1) का उपयोग किया जाता है। एक से अधिक variables उपयोग करना हमेशा बेहतर होता है, बशर्ते वे आपस में perfectly correlated न हों।

  • a) Mean per unit estimator

  • c) Ratio estimator
