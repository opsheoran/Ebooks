Stratified Random Sampling

Stratification means division into layers auxiliary information related to the character under study may be used to divided population into various groups such that,

  1. Units within each groups or us homogenous as possible.
  2. The group means as widely difference as possible.

Thus a population consisting of ‘N’ sampling units is divided into ‘k’ relatively homogenous mutually disjoint subgroups terms as strata of sizes \(N_1, N_2, \ldots, N_k\) such that \(\sum_{i=1}^k N_i = N\).

If a sample random sampling (generally without replacement) of sizes \(n_i\) (\(i = 1, 2, \ldots, k\)) is drawn from the stratum respectively such that \(\sum_{i=1}^k n_i = n\). This sample is termed as stratified sample of size ‘n’ and the technique of drawing such a sample is called stratified random sampling.

Sample Mean: \(\bar{y}_{st} = \sum_{i=1}^k W_i \bar{y}_i\)
where \(W_i = N_i/N\) is the stratum weight.
Theorem 4.1: If in every stratum the sample estimator \(\bar{y}_i\) is unbiased and samples are drawn independently in difference strata then \(\bar{y}_{st}\) is an unbiased estimator of the population mean and its sampling variance is given by:

\[V(\bar{y}_{st}) = \sum_{i=1}^k W_i^2 V(\bar{y}_i)\]

Allocation of Sample Size

There are four method of allocation of sample size is to different strata in a stratified sampling procedure:

1. Equal Allocation

In this method a total sample size ‘n’ is divided equally among all the strata, i.e., for the \(i^{th}\) stratum: \(n_i = n/k\).

2. Proportional Allocation

The procedure of allocation is very common in practice because of its simplicity when no other information except the total of number of units in the \(i^{th}\) stratum is available a allocation of a given sample of size ‘n’ to different strata it’s done in proportion to their sizes. i.e., in the \(i^{th}\) stratum:

\(n_i = \frac{n N_i}{N} = n W_i\)

3. Neyman’s Allocation

This allocation of the total sample size to strata is called minimum variance per unit among difference strata in the same and the size of the its fixed. Sample size is are allocated by:

\(n_i = \frac{n W_i S_i}{\sum W_i S_i}\)

4. Optimum Allocation

In this method of allocation a sample sizes ‘\(n_i\)’ in the respective strata are determine with the view to minimize variance for \(\bar{y}_{st}\) for a specified cost of containing the sample survey or to minimize the cost for a specified value of variance of (\(\bar{y}_{st}\)).

Comparison with Simple Random Sampling

The variance of estimate of population mean in simple random sampling is given by:

\(V(\bar{y}_n)_{srs} = (1 - f) \frac{S^2}{n}\)

In stratified sampling with proportional allocation:

\(V(\bar{y}_{st})_{prop} = (1 - f) \sum \frac{W_i S_i^2}{n}\)

The difference in the stratum means greater is the gain in precision stratified random sampling with proportional allocation over unstratified simple random sampling.
